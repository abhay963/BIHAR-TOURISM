Introduction

The Sher Shah Tomb is an important historical site located in Sasaram, Bihar. It is the mausoleum of Sher Shah Suri, the founder of the Suri Empire in North India. The tomb is renowned for its remarkable Mughal architecture and historical significance.

History

Constructed in the 16th century, the tomb is a grand monument built by Sher Shah Suri himself. It stands as a testament to the architectural prowess of the Suri dynasty and reflects the grandeur of Mughal architecture. The tomb is surrounded by a large tank and lush gardens, adding to its historical charm.

How to Reach

The Sher Shah Tomb is located in Sasaram, which is well-connected by road. The nearest railway station is Sasaram Junction, and the nearest airport is Gaya Airport. From these points, local transportation such as taxis and auto-rickshaws can be used to reach the tomb.

Opening Hours

The tomb is open to visitors from sunrise to sunset. It is advisable to visit during daylight hours to fully appreciate the architectural details and surroundings.

Additional Information

Visitors to the Sher Shah Tomb can explore the beautifully landscaped gardens and the large tank surrounding the tomb. The site also offers a peaceful environment for reflection and appreciation of historical architecture.
