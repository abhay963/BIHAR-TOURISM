Introduction

Maa Mundeshwari Temple, located in Kaimur district of Bihar, is one of the oldest Hindu temples in India. Dedicated to Lord Shiva and Shakti, the temple is known for its historical and religious significance.

History

The temple is believed to have been built during the Gupta period, around 3rd century AD, making it one of the oldest functional temples in the world. The temple's architecture reflects the Nagara style, and it has been a place of continuous worship for centuries.

How to Reach

Maa Mundeshwari Temple is well connected by road. The nearest town is Bhabua, from where regular buses and taxis are available. The closest railway station is Bhabua Road, and the nearest airport is in Varanasi, about 100 km away.

Opening Hours

The temple is open to visitors from 6:00 AM to 7:00 PM every day.

Additional Information

The temple is known for its annual Mundeshwari festival, which attracts thousands of devotees from all over the region. The temple is also famous for the continuous worship that has been carried out for centuries.
