Introduction

Jalmandir, nestled in the serene waters of Pawapuri in Bihar, is an exquisite Jain temple that stands as a beacon of spiritual reverence. Built in memory of Lord Mahavira, the 24th Tirthankara of Jainism, this temple is surrounded by a tranquil lotus pond, making it a picturesque destination. The sacred site attracts pilgrims and tourists alike, offering a peaceful retreat away from the hustle and bustle of everyday life.

History

The Jalmandir was constructed around 500 BCE in the exact place where Lord Mahavira attained nirvana. Legend has it that after the cremation of Lord Mahavira, a great demand for his ashes arose, leading to the construction of the temple amidst the waters to protect the sacred remains. The temple is a symbol of peace and spirituality, reflecting the profound teachings of Jainism.

How to Reach

Jalmandir is well-connected by road, rail, and air. The nearest airport is in Patna, approximately 85 kilometers away. Regular trains and buses also operate from Patna to Pawapuri. Visitors can easily reach the temple by hiring a taxi or taking local transport from the nearest city.

Opening Hours

The temple is open daily from 6:00 AM to 8:00 PM. Visitors are encouraged to visit early in the morning or late in the afternoon to experience the serene ambiance of the temple.
