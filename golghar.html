Introduction

Golghar, located in Patna, Bihar, is a large granary built by the British in 1786. It is an iconic structure with historical significance and offers a panoramic view of the city from its top.

History

Golghar was constructed after the famine of 1770, which claimed millions of lives. It was designed by Captain John Garstin as a preventive measure against future famines. The building is a marvel of engineering, with a unique beehive-shaped structure.

How to Reach

Golghar is located in the heart of Patna and is easily accessible by road. It is a popular tourist spot, well connected by public transport. The nearest railway station is Patna Junction, and the nearest airport is Jay Prakash Narayan Airport.

Opening Hours

Golghar is open to visitors from 10:00 AM to 5:00 PM every day. Visitors can climb the 145 steps to the top to enjoy a beautiful view of the Ganges and the surrounding cityscape.

Additional Information

Golghar is not only a historical landmark but also a place of great architectural interest. The site is popular among tourists and locals alike, especially for evening walks and photography.
