Introduction

Madhubani, located in the Mithila region of Bihar, is renowned worldwide for its unique and vibrant art form, Madhubani painting. The town is a cultural hub, known for its rich traditions and artistic heritage.

History

Madhubani painting, also known as Mithila painting, has its roots in ancient India. It is believed to have originated in the Mithila region around 2500 years ago. The paintings were traditionally created by women on the walls of their homes as a form of storytelling and expression.

How to Reach

Madhubani is well connected by road and rail. The nearest railway station is Madhubani Railway Station, and the nearest airport is Darbhanga Airport. The town is easily accessible from major cities in Bihar and neighboring states.

Opening Hours

While Madhubani is a town and not a single tourist attraction, visitors can explore the local markets, art galleries, and workshops throughout the day. There is no specific opening or closing time, but it is advisable to visit during daylight hours.

Additional Information

Madhubani is not only famous for its art but also for its cultural festivals, such as Chhath Puja and Dussehra. Visitors can immerse themselves in the local culture and experience the vibrant traditions of Mithila.
