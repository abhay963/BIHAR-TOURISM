Introduction

Valmiki National Park is a renowned wildlife sanctuary located in Bihar, known for its rich biodiversity and scenic beauty. It is named after the ancient sage Valmiki and is an important conservation area for various wildlife species.

History

The park was established to protect the habitat of the Bengal tiger and other endangered species. It has a rich history of conservation efforts and plays a crucial role in preserving the wildlife of the region.

How to Reach

Valmiki National Park is accessible by road from major cities in Bihar. The nearest railway station is Valmikinagar Junction, and the closest airport is Jay Prakash Narayan International Airport in Patna. Local transportation options like taxis and buses are available to reach the park.

Opening Hours

The park is open to visitors during the day from sunrise to sunset. It is recommended to visit with a guided tour to explore the wildlife and natural beauty effectively.

Additional Information

Visitors to Valmiki National Park can enjoy a variety of wildlife sightings, nature trails, and guided safaris. The park also offers opportunities for bird watching and nature photography.
