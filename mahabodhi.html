Introduction

The Mahabodhi Temple, located in Bodh Gaya, Bihar, is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and one of the most sacred places in Buddhism. It marks the location where Gautama Buddha attained enlightenment under the Bodhi Tree.

History

The Mahabodhi Temple was built during the 3rd century BCE by Emperor Ashoka. The temple complex has been an important pilgrimage site for Buddhists for centuries, and it continues to attract devotees from all over the world.

How to Reach

The Mahabodhi Temple is well connected by road, with Bodh Gaya being the nearest town. The closest railway station is Gaya Junction, and the nearest airport is Gaya Airport, about 12 km away.

Opening Hours

The temple is open to visitors from 5:00 AM to 9:00 PM every day.

Additional Information

The Mahabodhi Temple complex also includes the sacred Bodhi Tree, several stupas, and other structures of religious importance. The site is especially crowded during Buddhist festivals such as Buddha Purnima.
