Introduction

Nalanda was a renowned ancient center of learning in Bihar, India. It is one of the oldest universities in the world and was a major center of learning and Buddhist studies from the 5th to the 12th century CE.

History

The university was established during the Gupta dynasty and attracted scholars and students from all over the world. Nalanda's influence in education and culture spread across Asia and beyond, making it a key institution in the history of education.

How to Reach

Nalanda is located about 95 kilometers southeast of Patna. The nearest airport is Jay Prakash Narayan International Airport in Patna. It is well-connected by road and rail, making it easily accessible for visitors.

Opening Hours

Nalanda is open to visitors every day from 9:00 AM to 5:00 PM. It is recommended to visit early in the day to explore the vast ruins and the associated museum.

Additional Information

Nalanda is a UNESCO World Heritage site and continues to be a place of great historical and cultural significance. Visitors can explore the ancient ruins, the Nalanda Museum, and the nearby Nava Nalanda Mahavihara, which is an institute dedicated to research in Pali literature and Buddhism.
